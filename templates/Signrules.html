Sign Rules & Processing Guide

This page explains how each supported sign should be shown to the camera for clear recognition. For easier processing and more stable results, some of our signs are altered a little bit from standard hand shapes. Please follow the instructions below carefully.

Important

Use a plain background, keep only one hand inside the camera frame, keep your hand steady for a moment, and stay at a medium distance from the webcam.

How it works

The camera tracks your hand landmarks and compares your hand pose with the supported letter patterns. If your hand is clear and stable, the app predicts the matching letter and adds it to the word output.

Processing note

Some signs are intentionally adjusted a little for easy processing, cleaner understanding, and better consistency in front of the webcam. Follow the SignBridge version shown on this page.

Hand direction

Most shown letters should be done with the left hand. For C, use the right hand as instructed. Some letters also require showing the front side or back side of the hand.

Supported Letters

A, B, C, D, E, F, G, H, I, K, L, M, O, P, R, S, T, U, W, Y

Photo slots are included below.

A

Left Hand

Make a closed fist. Keep all fingers folded and keep the thumb resting naturally outside for a clear side shape.

B

Left Hand

Open all four fingers straight upward and keep the thumb folded inward for a clean flat-palm look.

C

Right Hand

Use the right hand. Curve the fingers and thumb to form a clear “C” shape. Keep the curve wide and visible to the camera.

D

Left Hand

Raise the index finger straight up and close the remaining fingers. Keep the thumb supporting the closed fingers.

E

Left Hand

Bend all fingers inward slightly so the hand looks compact. Keep the shape tight and steady.

F

Left Hand

Touch the thumb and index finger to make a small ring. Keep the middle, ring, and pinky fingers extended upward.

G

Left Hand

Keep the index finger and thumb extended sideways with the other fingers closed for a narrow pointing shape.

H

Left Hand

Extend the index and middle fingers together sideways. Close the ring and pinky fingers.

I

Left Hand

Close all fingers and raise only the pinky finger upward.

K

Left Hand

Open the index and middle fingers upward in a V shape and keep the thumb lifted between them for a clear K shape.

L

Left Hand

Raise the index finger upward and extend the thumb sideways to form an “L”. Keep the other fingers closed.

M

Left Hand

Close the hand and keep the thumb tucked under the front fingers. Keep the hand compact and clearly visible.

W

Left Hand

For easier processing, close all fingers except the pinky and thumb. Keep only the pinky and thumb visible and separated clearly.

O

Left Hand

Curve all fingers and thumb to make a round “O” shape. Keep the circle neat and centered in front of the camera.

P

Custom SignBridge Rule

Open two fingers downward. Keep the hand rotated so the two extended fingers clearly point down for easy detection.

R

Left Hand

Cross the index and middle fingers while keeping the remaining fingers closed.

S

Custom SignBridge Rule

Open the thumb sideways and keep all other fingers closed. Make sure the thumb side profile is clearly visible.

T

Left Hand

Form a fist and place your thumb between the index and middle finger, with the front of the hand facing outward.

U

Custom SignBridge Rule

Open the index finger, middle finger, and thumb. Show the front side of the hand clearly to the camera.

Y

Left Hand

Open the thumb and pinky while keeping the index, middle, and ring fingers closed.

Letters Under Process

The following letters are currently under process and are not yet supported in this version: J, Q, V, N, X, Z and any other letters not listed in the supported section above. These are under active improvement for better accuracy, motion handling, and camera stability.

Best Practices for Users

For better recognition

  • Keep your hand inside the center area of the webcam.
  • Do one sign at a time and hold it steady for a moment.
  • Use proper lighting and avoid dark backgrounds.
  • Do not move too fast while signing.

Important project note

SignBridge is built for clear recognition and user-friendly interaction. For that reason, some signs are adjusted slightly from standard forms so the system can process them more clearly and users can understand the required pose more easily.
